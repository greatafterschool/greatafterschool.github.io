Spring Robotics Tournament!

Nov 17th, 2014

Steve Putz came up with the brilliantly simple and logical idea that GAS Robotics should launch with a Spring tournament. This would not interfere with the FLL season (FLL is in the autumn to avoid colliding with FRC), and actually spring is a better-for-schools time of year. FLL always seemed backwards: you rush as soon as school starts and then do nothing (or I run a club) in the Spring.

Although very early in this process, if you are interested in hosting a 24 team tournament (or less, but 24 is pretty nice) or would love to participate in a Spring tournament somewhere in the Bay Area, please contact me or anyone who is part of Los Alto Robotics.

Suggested Format

The best tournament I ever attended was run by Los Alto Robotics and had some very nice characteristics:

  • It was simply ‘In the afternoon’ running from Noon to about 6PM. So you have lunch, go to tournament, get awards, go get dinner.
  • There was very little down time: teams ran through three pairs of tables and the judging pretty much continuously. There were gaps (it wasn’t a grind) but there weren’t significant breaks.
  • During judging deliberation, guest FRC teams could visit or teams could present their projects to each other

This was so pleasant that I would recommend it as a model. Also since there is no subsequent event (e.g. a ‘Regional’) judging may be faster or we could even simply drop the judging and just have kids present to everyone if they feel like it.

Venues

It would be nice if the venues were relatively consistent in format, but venues should be able to block out as many ‘home slots’ as they need. It is their venue after all.

Team Registration

Registration is done here:

  • http://www.meetup.com/NorCal-Lego-Robotics-League/

It is first-come first-served starting 28 days (or something similar) before the event. The cost per team could vary per venue due to facility costs.

Other Thoughts?

If you have any other thoughts or suggestions, just let me know and I will add them here. Or join the meetup which has a forums capability.
